The real

The Hard Moments

Every real relationship has moments that test it. Here are some of theirs — navigated, not avoided.

The "goodnight" misread
On May 5, Karan said goodnight after SS was buried in a work sheet and couldn't call. She texted back minutes later: "Sorry are u mad at me?" He wasn't. She asked anyway. That's how much she cares about getting it right.
Distance between trips
She was in Bhubaneswar, he was in Kolkata or Bengaluru. Multiple travel stretches through March and April meant they often went days without seeing each other — only voices and video calls through patchy hotel Wi-Fi.
The Serious Talk — April 28
Parents already knew. The morning after "I love you," SS brought up marriage, money, expectations, health, families. She said she'd been "mostly independent but broke." He said: "I just want us both to be secure, support each other's ambitions, save well, and build things together." She went into a meeting. He was already clear.
The scooter situation
Someone in her building kept vandalising her scooty. She posted on the building group: "Whoever is doing it, count your days." Karan said be diplomatic. She said: "Ye harassment hai Karan. I've been quiet about this long enough." He immediately said: bring the scooter to my place. Done.
Missing each other badly
"U know u miss someone so much that u get angry at them? Wo feeling." — SS, sitting in his apartment in Indiranagar, waiting for him to return from Kolkata. One of the cats was also visibly sad. She confirmed this.
Timing, always timing
He called, she was asleep. She called, his phone was dead. He passed out before she said "I love you" the first time. She found the missed call. He found her message at 12:31 AM and replied immediately. Timing was always their obstacle — and somehow also their proof.
